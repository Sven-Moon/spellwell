Dungeons & Dragons is a world of life and death based on dice rolls. As any experienced adventurure can attest, missing your opponent by rolling a 13 instead of the needed 14 can have severe consequences because it's all or nothing for damage and effect.

Your spells and attacks have varying "hit zones" on the die that range anywhere from 20 - 2, all the way down to just 20 that varies with target and what spell save mechanic is attached to the spell. If fate has decided you're going to roll that 13, you're getting the 13. If you're casting a spell that requires a 13+, you hit, but if you had instead cast a spell with a 14+ requirement, you miss. Same roll, different oucomes. You dig? Great.

What the average player doesn't have, though, is the knowledge of what works better and worse for a given target. Well, in that reguard, this app helps you to step beyond the average by indicating which spell save type is the most likely to hit.

Do you see a lot of orcs? Input their stats to discover that they are most vulnerable to spells requiring intelligence saving throws, followed by spell attacks (requiring AC saving throws).

AND, if you put in your own character stats (level and spell modifier), you get even better information: your % chance to hit and your potential success die rolls. Does it seem like a particular monster is ALWAYS dodging your dex spells? Well, if your hit percentage is 45%, you can expect to miss more than half the time so you're not unlucky, you're just a victim of poor decisions.

Go to 5e.tools where you can find the stats of every listed official creature known to the 5e D&D universe.

Careful! Don't put in their stat, which ranges from 0 - 30. Instead, put in their modifier for the stat, which is listed in parenthesis beside the stat. So it'll look something like 11(+1) or 7(-2).

I do it this way because in-game it's easier for the user to determine the modifier than the stat. Seeing a miss on roll of 12 versus a DC 13 spell save means at most a +0 to the stat. Determining the actual stat requires a second calculation and can only resolve to a range, anyway (that is a +1 could mean either 11 or 12... but who cares about the difference?).

You might see it that way. Hell, I the creator of this tool might see it that way. As an alternative, you can use this app as a character log of known attributes.

Throw a DC 15 con-save spell at them. DM rolls a 15. Does it stick? Yeah? Great, now you know that the creature's constitution spell save is +0 or lower.

A friend fires off a 12 DC dex-save spell. The DM rolls a 16 and saves. Well, crap. You now know the creature has at least a +4 dex save.

Note that if your DM only calls out hit or miss without allowing you to see the dice result, it isn't possible to get this information.

There may still be some question as to how a character could possibly know exactly how resistant to a spell type a monster is just by seeing a hit or miss out on the battlefield. But I think you could reasonably argue that you have some knowledge of your own and your companions' skill at said spell, and thus seeing a hit or a miss would impart the same kind of knowledge as a calculated hit/miss percentage imbues. Imagine a creature that manages to avoid your friend's spell even though they didn't appear to even notice the spell was coming at them versus a creature that does a twisting backflip to avoid the spell. The first probably has a -2 modifier, but happened to roll 2 better than they needed, while the latter probably has a +2 and a +6 spell save.

And remember, just because you know that a spell has a 70% chance to hit, doesn't mean you know the outcome prior to rolling the die.

The red bar signifies at least 65% chance to hit. This is the recommended chance to hit to be competitive in a 1-1 fight with a monster. Say you were in a fight with a 50% chance to hit. Well, you'd be hitting half the time and so would they. All other things equal, you'd die as often as you lived, which isn't very good. That 15% boost is enough to give you a better-than-random chance at killing your foes before they kill you.
